Appendix A. Foreign Conjunction

The conjunction !: applies to integer scalar left and right arguments to produce verbs, with the exception that the case 5!:0 produces an adverb. These verbs behave like any other verb: they may be assigned names, may serve as arguments to adverbs and conjunctions, and must be used with an argument even though it may have no significance (whence an empty string must be the argument, as in 7!:0 ''). Where these verbs take names as arguments, the names are boxed, as in 4!:55 'a';'bc' to erase the names a and bc . A bracketed left argument indicates that it is optional.
